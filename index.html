I am Eva Designer

Integrating Writing and Design Portfolio

Design Beginnings


Design Fuego

Design Fuego is an introductory project where I learned how to navigate code and digital image formatting. The end product was a webpage with a self introduction and a reflection on my past experiences with design.

Logo A/B/C

I experimented with Canva and Adobe to design logos to advertize a potential business/product. It was a challenge designing logos that were simutaneously creative and representative of myself as well as professional looking.

UX Testing Logo Project

I conducted a UX research project to test the effectiveness of my logos. After interviewing three participants, I designed a final logo based on the feedback I recieved.

Cartoon Portrait

I used Adobe Illustrator to create a cartoon portrait of myself. This project taught me how to work Adobe Illustrator and allowed me to become more comfortable using adobe software.

Good Design

For this project, I combined what I knew about good design with my Adobe Indesign skills. I used the Indesign software to create a page that analyzed the features of a successful design.

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

When making my projects, I used rhetoric, technology, and ethics as tools and points of guidance during the process. The way I chose to write and communicate, the rules and guidelines I followed, and the software that I used helped me eloquently execute my designs. Although my portfolio belongs to me, I had to consider what my target audience was looking for when designing it in order maximize effectiveness. For example, when working on my UX research project, I had to be mindful of the both language and approach I used while conducting the actual interview as well as in the slideshow. This ensures I get reliable responses and that the outcome of such responses can be adequatly communicated.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

To produce effective designs, it was impotant for me to understand basic design principles. Variables such as composition, color, balance, and clarity directly influenced the legibility and accessability of my designs in my portfolio. Understanding and enacting proper design principles allowed me to efficiently communicate my messages and goals with the user in the final product. For the logo project, cartoon portrait, and good design study, understanding design principles was vital when creating my own designs. I learned quickly that applying design principles was much more difficult than it was being able to recognize them in other peoples work. I found looking at exemplar designs beneficial when putting together designs of my own.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

Engaging in a workshop environment gave me the oppurtunity to learn more about my own design process by exposing me to the processes of my peers. Giving and recieivng feedback helped figure out the best approach moving forward. When I was going back and refining my portfolio, I incorporated the feedback my peers gave me and considered how they approached their projects when finalizing my designs. I found peer feedback to be most beneficial during the logo project. I had some trouble trying to come with ideas that looked professional, so I found seeing other peoples logos and getting their feedback to be helpful.

4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Revision was a vital step when working on my portfolio. As someone relativly new to media design, a lot of the initial choices I made were not the most effective. The more experience I gained and feedback I recieved, the better I understood design principes and processes and was then able to revise my designs accordingly. I had to revise my design fuego project the most. Coming into this course, I was very unfamilar with code and webdesign. As I learned more, I had to go back several time and make proper adjustments.

5. Learn to present your work.

I learned that how well my work presents itself in a public or published setting is what matters most. Even if I like and understand my portfolio, my designs can not do their jobs if they are not accessible to an audience. While designing my portfolio, I had to consider how it would be percieved by outsiders. It was difficult trying to put myself in the perspective of the user since I will never be able to experience my products in such a way. Hence, learning about the rules and guidelines and getting peer feedback allowed me to better predict how people would interact with my portfolio. When presenting my UX research project to my peers, I had to take into account what they did and did not know going into the presentation. I acknowledge that I am much more familiar with my own designs than they are, so it was important for me to take time to prepare ahead of time to ensure I addressed what was necessary.
